Rome

Rome was the capital of the Roman Empire, which became one of the most powerful civilizations in history. It is known for its achievements in government, law, architecture, and engineering, such as aqueducts and the Colosseum. Today, Rome is the capital of Italy and home to important cultural and religious sites, including the Vatican.

Florence

Florence was the birthplace of the Renaissance, a period of great cultural, artistic, and intellectual growth in Europe. The city is famous for figures like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Dante, who shaped art and literature. Today, Florence remains a major center of history and tourism, known for landmarks like the Duomo and the Uffizi Gallery.

Milan

Milan is a major city in northern Italy known as a global center for fashion, design, and business. During the Renaissance, it was an important hub for art and culture, with contributions from artists like Leonardo da Vinci. Today, Milan is famous for landmarks such as the Milan Cathedral (Duomo) and La Scala opera house.

Venice

Venice is a unique Italian city built on canals, earning it the nickname "The Floating City." During the Middle Ages and Renaissance, it was a powerful center of trade and maritime dominance. Today, Venice is world-famous for its gondolas, St. Mark’s Basilica, and annual Carnival.
